From spreadsheets to AI workflows

I started my career in sales in India in 2014, managing B2B accounts and learning first-hand that most of the work wasn't the actual selling — it was the admin around it. The follow-up emails, the CRM updates, the reports that took half a Friday to put together. I got good at finding shortcuts, and that habit stuck.

After moving to Canada, I joined goeasy and worked my way up from analyst to Collections Manager, leading a team of ten. The job was performance-heavy — cure rates, scorecards, call volumes — and I became obsessed with finding ways to do more without burning the team out. Power Automate. Excel macros. CRM workflow redesigns. If there was a repetitive process eating hours, I was looking for a way to compress it.

The shift toward AI was a natural extension of that mindset. When tools like ChatGPT and Microsoft Copilot started landing in corporate inboxes, I watched two things happen in parallel: some people dismissed them, and others started quietly using them to get twice as much done. I wanted to be in the second group — and I wanted to help my team get there too.

Why I built a training practice

I ran internal pilot workshops at goeasy — informal sessions where I showed teammates how to use AI to draft CRM notes, summarise email threads, and build first-draft reports. The feedback was consistent: why didn't someone teach us this earlier? The tools were there. The time-savings were obvious. The missing piece was someone to bridge the gap between what AI can do and what a specific team actually needs.

That's what I do now. I work with business teams — operations, collections, sales, admin — to figure out where AI fits into their actual workflow, then build the skills and habits to make it stick. Not generic demos. Not theoretical frameworks. Prompts that work on real tasks, starting Monday.

What I care about

I'm obsessed with measurable outcomes. Hours saved, error rates reduced, reports that used to take 45 minutes now taking 8. I'm also serious about responsible use — every workshop includes a clear module on what not to share with AI tools and how to verify output before it goes anywhere. AI that creates compliance risk isn't useful; AI that's fast and safe is transformative.

Outside of work: cricket, A.R. Rahman on shuffle, and the occasional Lightroom edit. I live in the GTA with my wife and genuinely believe that the best workflows leave room for the stuff that matters.
